Blue Cross Blue Shield of Alabama
Health insurance payer
Consumers in economic uncertainty defaulted to transactional thinking about health insurance.
Context

Blue Cross Blue Shield of Alabama is the state's largest health insurer, serving commercial, Medicare, and individual marketplace members across Alabama. The engagement came during a period of sustained healthcare and economic volatility, when consumer trust in health insurance was eroding and emotional loyalty was difficult to build or maintain. The campaign needed to reach distinctly different audiences simultaneously — individual members, families, small business owners making coverage decisions on behalf of employees, and Medicare-eligible adults whose awareness of BCBS had softened.

Solution

David contributed to a multi-channel campaign spanning broadcast, digital, mobile, and display that used psychological principles including authority, reciprocity, and social proof to shift BCBS from a transactional product to a brand associated with care continuity. Digital executions integrated specific plan tools including Teladoc, the Dr. Finder platform, and wellness programs, driving actual member engagement rather than awareness alone. The B2B track framed the BCBS card as a valued employee retention asset for small business owners, directly addressing the commercial insurance buyer's decision criteria. The campaign delivered a Net Promoter Score three to four times the national average, positioning BCBS of Alabama as the top-ranked Blue Cross plan in the country.

OhioHealth Cancer
Community health system cancer program
A community health system was invisible in cancer care against a dominant academic competitor.
Context

OhioHealth is a community-based health system in Columbus, Ohio, competing in a cancer care market where Ohio State University's James Cancer Hospital held commanding brand recognition as an academic medical center with built-in research credibility. At the time of the engagement, 52% of OhioHealth's own associates and 83% of surveyed consumers named the James first when thinking about cancer care. The system was simultaneously launching a new Blood and Marrow Transplant inpatient unit, requiring a campaign that could build category credibility while managing the psychological complexity of cancer messaging for a community health system without an academic designation.

Solution

David contributed to a campaign strategy built on the "Keep Making Plans" framework, which reframed the patient experience around life after treatment — focusing on survivorship and continued living rather than the clinical intensity of a diagnosis. The strategy deployed grassroots experiential activations with consumer brands including Jeni's Ice Cream and Sherwin-Williams, intercepting target audiences in familiar community settings to reduce the intimidation associated with specialized cancer care. Hyper-targeted audience personas, including "Ohio Metro Persons of Color" and working-age cancer patient profiles, supported tailored paid media, community PR, and in-clinic programming such as a milestone bell-ringing initiative and patient-facing Spotify playlists.

Moffitt Cancer Center
NCI-designated Comprehensive Cancer Center
Moffitt treated only 5% of Florida cancer patients despite clinical outcomes far above average.
Context

Moffitt Cancer Center is a freestanding NCI-designated Comprehensive Cancer Center in Tampa, Florida, operating without the built-in primary care referral networks that most academic medical systems depend on. Despite posting survival rates up to four times the national average for certain cancers, Moffitt was reaching a small fraction of eligible patients, with affluent Floridians defaulting to legacy institutions in New York and Boston and many patients arriving only after receiving an incorrect diagnosis elsewhere. A new CEO brought a mandate for bolder brand positioning and expanded audience development.

Solution

David contributed to a brand strategy anchored in "Urgency" and "Boldness," building audience-specific messaging tracks for symptomatic patients, prevention audiences, researchers, and donors. The strategy for symptomatic patients emphasized the clinical risk of receiving initial treatment at a non-specialized facility, using accessible analogies to make the case for seeking definitive care first. The campaign also supported Moffitt's insurance network strategy by generating patient demand strong enough to maintain favorable positioning with Medicare Advantage plans using selective contracting. The resulting campaign delivered a 66.5% year-over-year increase in new patient conversions and a 40% lift in digital engagement.
